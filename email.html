EMAIL

In this category we will look how you can protect yourself from malicious emails. Because of how little security email has at its core, it has become the most popular way for scammers, hackers and other people with bad intentions to gain money, personal information, or access.

Some of the techniques used to reach their goals are phishing scams, classic scams, spreading of malware, and tracking of emails.

We will give a brief rundown of how these techniques work, and what you can do to recognize them and keep your safety intact.

PHISHING

Phishing emails are emails that trick you into giving away confidential or private information such as passwords, Bank ID, PIN numbers, etc.

The most common way this takes place, is that you get an email that tells you to go to a website and enter your information. This email might look like it's from a legit source with all the graphics and correct sender's address, but this website will log all the information you type in.

They might not use the information for a while either so it can be hard to pinpoint when you gave away the information.

HOW TO AVOID PHISHING SCAMS

  • Don't click links in emails. Visit the website manually and go to the service you need.
  • Never give away personal information over email.
  • If you suspect you have been the victim of a phishing attack, make sure to change your password everywhere you use the same password.
  • Check the reply to field. It is rarely used outside of scams.

EMAIL SCAMS

Email is a very simple way to reach a lot of people, because of this it has become a invaluable tool for scammers. These scams usually work by the scammer claiming to have a large fortune, but it's unaccessible and the only way to gain access is to pay. Another popular scam are romance scams. These scams will make it seem like someone is very interested in getting to know you. If you reply to this email you will probably be asked to pay for a plane ticket or similar so they can come visit you.

What applies to all scams is that they either want your personal information or money. Another thing the scams have in common, is that they use Social Engineering as the main tool. This means that they manipulate the victim and plays on psychological factors like: greed, loneliness, and trust.

To elaborate, social engineering is essentially to hack people. In order to get access to something, they will contact someone who can help provide this, and pretend to be someone who is authorized to access it. In short, social engineering can be used to make your life very hard.

HOW TO RECOGNIZE EMAIL SCAMS

  • If something seems to good to be true, it probably is.
  • Use a search engine to search for a snippet of the text to see if it's a known scam.
  • Look for spelling mistakes (scammers will often use translating applications.)

SPOOFED EMAILS

A spoofed email, is an email with a forged senders address. Because email at it's core doesn't have any authentication it is really simple to forge a senders address. It can be done by visiting various websites or through applications written for this use. An example of how spoofed emails can be used are to make it seem like an email is from a friend. The email would then contain malware that looks for email addresses on your system. Say someone finds the email addresses of two friends, Rick and Jenna. It can then send an infected email to Jenna disguised as a email from Rick and continue this chain from Jennas computer.

There's something called SPF (Sender Policy Framework). This is a email-validation system made to prevent spoofing. It is unfortunately not used all that much at this time, despite being very easy to set up. The email would be likely to end up in the spam folder, rather than the inbox if SPF is in place.

WAYS TO IDENTIFY FAKE EMAILS

  • Be skeptical of the language used; is the sender of the email writing in the same language and style that a person would?
  • If possible verify the content with the sender.
  • Never assume email is safe. Be skeptical what links you click or what information you share, and who you share it with.

EMAIL TRACKING

In some cases it can be beneficial for criminals to track emails. By tracking emails you can gain access to information about who is reading it, when it was read, and where you are when you read it.

They can get the who and when by simply using the functionality to confirm when an email has been delivered, and the function to add a confirmation when the mail has been opened. There are a couple of ways they can get your location. They can add a unique link that tracks the IP-address of the person visiting, or they can add a picture in the email that has to be downloaded from a server where they can store the IP-address of the people downloading.
